From Tokens to Taps: An Annotated History of Paying for the Subway

The current turnstile dates back to 1994 and takes tokens and MetroCards. This turnstile communicates with a computer in the station as well as a terminal in the booth. It will now communicate with the OMNY system, too.

The MTA's newest payment method is OMNY, which allows contactless payments through prepaid OMNY cards, credit cards or a smart device such as an iPhone or Apple Watch.

These turnstile screens display messages when the Metrocard is swiped through the reader. Going forward, OMNY will display confirmation messages on the larger screen.

Here's the old token slot on these MTA turnstiles. Tokens were introduced in 1953 in place of nickels and were in use until 2003.

Metrocards were introduced in 1992 as a magnetic stripe card used for fare payment on transportation in the New York City area. They replaced the previous system of subway tokens.
